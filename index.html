Description

Lebanon is a captivating country in the Middle East, boasting a rich history and vibrant culture. From the bustling nightlife of Beirut to the ancient ruins of Baalbek, there is no shortage of cultural attractions to explore. Its stunning coastline and rugged mountains offer opportunities for both relaxation and adventure. Visitors can indulge in delicious Lebanese cuisine and immerse themselves in the warm hospitality of the locals. However, it is essential to stay informed about the current situation and adhere to any travel advisories for a safe and enjoyable trip.

Transportation

Transportation in Lebanon primarily relies on a combination of public and private options. The country has a well-connected road network, with buses and shared taxis (known as "service") being common modes of public transportation. Taxis are also widely available, both shared and private. Beirut has a public bus system, and intercity buses connect major cities. However, traffic congestion can be a challenge, particularly in urban areas. It is advisable to plan travel in advance and consider using private car services or renting a car for more convenience and flexibility.
